Every member in Kanri has a **member number** — a unique numeric code they can use to sign in at your school's [kiosk](/kiosk/).

## What is a member number?

A member number is a short numeric ID, up to 6 digits (for example, 483920), that uniquely identifies a member within your school.

Member numbers exist so members have a quick, reliable way to sign in at the kiosk. Instead of typing their full name, they can enter their number — which is faster and avoids issues with name spellings. Members can also search by first name if they prefer.

## How member numbers are assigned

Kanri automatically generates a unique member number when a new member profile is created. You don't need to do anything — the number is assigned instantly.

Member numbers are random within the 0–999,999 range and are unique within your school. No two active members will have the same number.

:::note
Member numbers cannot currently be edited or reassigned after they are created. If a member asks to change their number, this isn't possible at this time.
:::

## Where to find a member's number

The member number is always visible in the member's profile header, displayed as:

> **First Last - [number]**

For example: **Marcus Webb - 120483**

You'll also see the number in the member overview cards and at the top of each sub-page (Attendance, Notes, Interactions, etc.).

## How members use their number at the kiosk

At the kiosk sign-in screen, members are prompted to "Search by your first name". The on-screen keyboard includes both letters and numbers. A member can either:

- Type their **first name** — the kiosk shows all members whose first or full name starts with those letters.
- Type their **member number** — the kiosk looks up the member with that exact number.

Results appear as a list showing each matching member's full name and their number (for example, "Marcus Webb - 120483"). The member taps their name, then taps **Next** to proceed to class selection.

Only members with an active membership appear in the kiosk search results.

If a member forgets their number, they can still sign in by searching their first name.
