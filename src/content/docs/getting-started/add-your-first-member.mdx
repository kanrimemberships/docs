**Members** are the students at your school. This guide walks through adding someone from scratch and getting them properly set up: profile, membership, and ready for billing.

:::note
The Members list shows everyone in your school's database, including contacts (parents/guardians), staff, and students. Kanri assigns each person a type automatically based on their role. When you're just getting started, don't worry about this — add your students and Kanri will keep them organised.
:::

## Before you start

You'll need at least one [program](/getting-started/create-your-first-program/) set up before you can assign a membership.

## Step 1: Create the member profile

1. Go to **Members** in the main navigation.
2. Click **New Member**.
3. Fill in the member's details:
   - **First name** and **Last name** (required)
   - **Family status**: the member's marital or family situation (Single, Married, Common Law, etc.) — optional
   - **Sex**: optional
   - **Birthday**: used for the [Birthday report](/reports/other/birthdays/) and age-based filtering
   - **Phone number**: used for SMS messages
   - **Email address**: used for login, receipts, and messages
   - **Can manage self**: turn this on if the member should be able to log in to the [Member Portal](/member-portal/) to view their own profile, attendance, and documents
4. Click **Save**.

:::tip
You don't need to fill in everything right now. You can always [edit the member](/members/edit-member/) later to add missing details.
:::

## Step 2: Add a contact (for junior members)

If the member is a child or has a parent or guardian who should receive communications or be associated with their account, add them as a contact.

1. Open the member's profile and go to the **Contacts** tab.
2. Click **New Contact**.
3. Fill in the contact's details. You can either create a new person or link to someone who is already a member in the system:
   - **First Name, Last Name, Email, Phone**: the contact's details (when creating a new person).
   - **Relationship**: how they're related to the member (Parent, Child, Sibling, Spouse, Friend, or Other).
   - **Can Manage**: turn this on if the contact should be able to manage the member's account, such as making payments on their behalf.
   - **Receive Communications**: turn this on if the contact should receive messages sent to this member.
4. Click **Save**.

See [Contacts](/members/contacts/) for more detail on how contacts work.

## Step 3: Assign a membership

A [membership](/members/memberships/) is an enrolment record that links a member to a program. Without one, the member can't check in at the kiosk or appear in program-based reports.

1. Go to the **Memberships** tab on the member's profile.
2. Click **New Membership**.
3. Select the **Program** to enrol them in.
4. Select their starting **Level** (if your program uses levels).
5. Set the **Status**. This defaults to your school's active membership status, so you usually won't need to change it.
6. Optionally, enter a **Sessions Remaining** count if this membership has a fixed number of sessions. Leave it blank for unlimited.
7. Click **Save**.

## Member numbers and kiosk check-in

Every member is automatically assigned a unique 6-digit number when their profile is created. Members can use this number — or search by first name — to sign in at the [kiosk](/kiosk/). See [Member Number](/members/member-number/) for more detail.

The number is displayed at the top of the member's profile. Members can quote it to staff, or look it up themselves on the [Member Portal](/member-portal/).
